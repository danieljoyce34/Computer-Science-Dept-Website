Why Consider a Computer Science Major?

Which field has...

...the third best-rated job in the world, and 4 of the top 10 highest paid, highest growth jobs?

...3 of the top 7 jobs in highest demand?

...a looming severe shortage in college graduates?

Computer Science!

Computer Science (CS) jobs are among the highest paid, highest satisfaction jobs of the projected highest growth jobs through 2020. With this constantly growing field, the demand for CS majors is persistently increasing; however, the supply is not meeting the demand. This severe shortage of CS graduates looms and promises excellent opportunities for savvy students.

Noteworthy Facts:

  • Software engineers rank third on Forbes' list of best jobs for 2013.
  • Four computing jobs are in the top ten of the US News World Report's list of the 100 best jobs of 2013 based on highest salary and highest growth.
    • 4. Computer Systems Analyst: $78,770 (median annual salary)
    • 6. Database Administrator: $75,190
    • 7. Software Developer: $89,280
    • 9. Web Developer: $77,990
  • According to US World News Report's list of jobs with the highest hiring demand, Computer Science jobs rank in 3 of the top 7 as of 2013.
  • The Bureau of Labor Statistics projects that there will be 4.2 million jobs in computing and information technology by the year 2020 in the U.S., putting these fields among the fastest growing occupational fields.
  • The Bureau of Labor Statistics also projects that 51,474 students are to graduate with a Computer Science degree. Simultaneously, 122,300 jobs requiring a degree in Computer Science are expected to become available. These trends are predicted to last at least until 2020.

Visit the ACM Computing Degrees and Careers site for more information and resources about the exciting field of computing.

Consider the decreasing supply and increasing demand. Choose Computer Science as a major.
